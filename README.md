# The Constitutional Civic Field: A Structural Account of How a Republic Carries as One Public Order

## What is this?
A foundational civics artifact in the Structural Flow library.

## Why should I care?
This artifact asks whether constitutional civic order is a real held field or only a pile of important but separate things like courts, statutes, records, legitimacy conditions, and public responses.

## What is the claim?
The claim is that constitutional civic order is a real held structural field: the condition under which a republic ratchets person-originated civic signals into recognized, memory-bearing, proportionate public constraint while preserving the person as the primary constitutional boundary.

## What is this not?
Not a replacement for constitutional law.
Not a policy platform.
Not merely Constitutional Physiology under another name.
Not a theory of civics in full.

## How was this done?
By deriving the civic field under the existing Structural Flow hinge grammar and then showing:
- what the field is
- what it is not
- how the person functions as the primary constitutional boundary
- what field-level failure looks like
- where Constitutional Physiology fits as the articulation layer
